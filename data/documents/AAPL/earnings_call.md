# AAPL Q1 FY2025 Earnings Call Transcript (Excerpts)

## CEO Remarks — Tim Cook

"We are pleased to report record revenue for the quarter, driven by strong iPhone 16 demand across all regions. Services reached an all-time high, with over 1 billion paid subscriptions globally. Our installed base continues to grow, which is the foundation for our long-term business model."

"With respect to China, we saw double-digit revenue decline year over year, which we attribute to a combination of market saturation, intensified local competition from Huawei and others, and macroeconomic softness. We continue to invest in our presence there and are working to address the competitive dynamics."

"On the AI front, Apple Intelligence features are now live on iPhone 16 models and select iPad and Mac models. User engagement with these features has been encouraging, and we see it as a meaningful driver of upgrade cycles over the next several years."

## CFO Remarks — Luca Maestri

"Gross margin for the quarter was 46.9%, up 60 basis points year over year, reflecting favorable mix shift toward Services and operating leverage in our supply chain."

"Foreign exchange headwinds reduced revenue by approximately 150 basis points. We expect this headwind to persist in the near term given the current dollar strength."

"We returned over $30 billion to shareholders during the quarter through dividends and share repurchases. Our balance sheet remains strong with net cash of approximately $45 billion."

## Q&A Session

**Analyst question on China:** "Given the continued weakness in China, can you give us more color on whether this is a cyclical issue or a structural one?"

**Tim Cook:** "We believe it is primarily cyclical. The China market has historically been subject to swings, and we have navigated them before. That said, we are paying close attention to the competitive landscape, particularly at the premium tier where Huawei is increasingly active. We are also mindful of geopolitical risks and their potential impact on our supply chain and market access."

**Analyst question on App Store regulation:** "How are you thinking about the EU DMA impact on App Store revenues?"

**Luca Maestri:** "The DMA has introduced new requirements that allow alternative app marketplaces in the EU. We have complied with these requirements. The financial impact is difficult to quantify precisely, but we expect it to be manageable. We remain confident in the value of the App Store ecosystem and our ability to compete effectively even in a more open environment."

**Analyst question on AI monetization:** "Can you give us a framework for how Apple Intelligence will translate to financial results?"

**Tim Cook:** "We see Apple Intelligence primarily as an upgrade cycle catalyst. Features like writing tools, image generation, and the enhanced Siri will drive users to upgrade to compatible devices. We are not currently monetizing AI features separately — they are part of the core product value proposition. Longer term, we see potential in AI-enhanced Services, but it is early days."
