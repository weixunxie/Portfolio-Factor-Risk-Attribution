# MSFT Q2 FY2025 Earnings Call Transcript (Excerpts)

## CEO Remarks — Satya Nadella

"This was a strong quarter driven by the continued momentum of Microsoft Cloud, which surpassed $40 billion in quarterly revenue for the first time. Azure grew 31% year over year, with AI services contributing approximately 13 points of that growth. We are seeing broad-based demand across industries and geographies."

"Copilot is now deeply integrated across our entire product portfolio — from Microsoft 365 Copilot to GitHub Copilot, Azure AI, and Dynamics. We added more than 100 new Copilot capabilities this quarter alone. The commercial Copilot seat additions continue to accelerate as enterprises move from pilot to broad deployment."

"On the security front, we have made our Secure Future Initiative the highest priority across the company. We are investing over $4 billion annually in cybersecurity, and we have made structural changes to how security is embedded in our engineering practices."

## CFO Remarks — Amy Hood

"Revenue for the quarter was $69.6 billion, up 12% year over year. Operating income grew 17% to $31.7 billion, with operating margin expanding to 45.6%."

"Capital expenditures were $20.4 billion, reflecting continued investment in our AI and cloud infrastructure. We expect capex to remain elevated throughout the fiscal year as we build capacity to meet customer demand."

"We returned $9 billion to shareholders through dividends and buybacks. Our strong free cash flow generation provides flexibility to continue investing in growth while returning capital."

## Q&A Session

**Analyst question on Azure growth sustainability:** "Azure has been accelerating, but can you sustain 30%+ growth as the base gets larger?"

**Amy Hood:** "We feel good about the demand signals we are seeing. The shift from on-premises to cloud is still in early innings for many enterprise segments, and AI workloads are net new capacity demands. We are not guiding for a specific growth rate, but I would say the demand environment is as strong as it has ever been."

**Analyst question on AI margin profile:** "When do AI services become accretive to margins?"

**Satya Nadella:** "AI is a massive infrastructure cycle, similar to the buildout of cloud capacity a decade ago. The capex we are spending now will generate revenue for years. Near term, there is margin pressure from infrastructure investment, but as AI workloads scale and we improve model efficiency, we expect AI services to converge toward cloud margins over time."

**Analyst question on cybersecurity incidents:** "Has the security disclosure process and the CSRB report affected sales conversations with government customers?"

**Satya Nadella:** "It is not a positive. We have been transparent about what happened and are taking it very seriously. Our government customers have constructive relationships with us and are working through the implications. Our Secure Future Initiative is a direct response and we are committed to making Microsoft the most secure platform in the industry."
