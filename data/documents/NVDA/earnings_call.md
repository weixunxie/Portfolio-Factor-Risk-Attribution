# NVDA Q3 FY2025 Earnings Call Transcript (Excerpts)

## CEO Remarks — Jensen Huang

"Demand for NVIDIA Blackwell is extraordinary. We are shipping to every major cloud service provider and a growing number of enterprise customers globally. The age of AI is in full swing, and NVIDIA is at the epicenter of this transformation."

"Our Data Center revenue reached $30.8 billion this quarter, up 112% year over year. Blackwell GPU shipments ramped faster than any product in our history. We have generated over $60 billion in Data Center revenue in the last four quarters, and the trajectory continues upward."

"Blackwell is not just a chip — it is a computing platform. From the NVLink switch to the NVL72 rack-scale systems, we are delivering integrated solutions that set the standard for AI infrastructure. Our software stack, including CUDA, cuDNN, TensorRT, and NIM microservices, creates a complete ecosystem that is extremely difficult to replicate."

"On China: we are fully complying with US export regulations. The H20 continues to sell well in China, and we are working to serve our customers in that market within the bounds of applicable regulations."

## CFO Remarks — Colette Kress

"Q3 revenue was $35.1 billion, up 94% year over year and above our guidance range. Gross margin was 74.6%, consistent with our expectations given the product mix ramp of Blackwell."

"We returned $11.2 billion to shareholders in the quarter through share repurchases. We have $7.5 billion remaining under our share repurchase authorization."

"For Q4, we guide revenue of $37.5 billion, plus or minus 2%. Gross margins are expected to be approximately 73%, reflecting continued ramp of Blackwell systems and integration of high-bandwidth memory."

## Q&A Session

**Analyst question on supply constraints:** "How long do you expect supply to remain constrained for Blackwell?"

**Jensen Huang:** "Demand significantly exceeds supply right now, and we expect supply to catch up over the course of 2025 as our manufacturing partners scale capacity. We are working very closely with TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix, and our CoWoS packaging partners. It is a complex supply chain to manage at this scale."

**Analyst question on custom silicon competition:** "Major hyperscalers are investing heavily in custom chips. How do you think about this risk to NVIDIA's share?"

**Jensen Huang:** "Custom chips are real, and our customers do use them for specific workloads. But the scope of what NVIDIA provides — from training to inference, from large language models to multimodal models to agentic AI — requires a programmable platform that is hard to replicate with fixed-function silicon. We view CUDA's 30 million developer ecosystem as our strongest competitive moat."

**Analyst question on export controls:** "Are you concerned about further export control tightening?"

**Colette Kress:** "We cannot predict regulatory actions. We work closely with the US government on these matters. The restrictions we have seen to date have been manageable, but we acknowledge that further restrictions would impact our ability to serve customers in affected markets, including China."
