# AMZN Q3 2024 Earnings Call Transcript (Excerpts)

## CEO Remarks — Andy Jassy

"This was an excellent quarter across the board. AWS revenue grew 19% year over year to $27.5 billion. North America retail operating income reached a record, reflecting the structural cost improvements we have made to our fulfillment network over the past two years."

"AWS is our most important long-term growth driver, and the AI opportunity within cloud is larger than anything we have seen since the original shift to cloud computing. Every customer we talk to is at some stage of their AI journey, and the vast majority are choosing AWS as their platform."

"We are investing heavily in AI across multiple layers: custom silicon (Trainium, Inferentia), foundation models via Bedrock, and AI-native applications built on top of the stack. Amazon Q for business and developers has gained significant traction among enterprise customers."

"On logistics: we are achieving the fastest delivery speeds in our history. Same-day delivery is now available in dozens of markets, and one-day delivery has become the standard for Prime customers. Speed is a competitive moat that is very difficult to replicate."

## CFO Remarks — Brian Olsavsky

"Total Q3 net sales were $159 billion, up 11% year over year. Operating income was $17.4 billion, up 56% year over year — a strong expansion that reflects AWS margin improvement and retail efficiency gains."

"Capital expenditures were $22.6 billion, and we expect to continue investing heavily, with total 2025 capex expected to significantly exceed 2024 levels as we build AI infrastructure. We are confident in the long-term returns on these investments."

"Free cash flow for the trailing twelve months was $47.7 billion, a record. We ended the quarter with $88 billion in cash and short-term investments."

## Q&A Session

**Analyst question on AWS AI competition vs. Azure:** "Microsoft has been very vocal about Azure's AI momentum. How do you think about the competitive dynamic?"

**Andy Jassy:** "Every major cloud provider will win a significant share of the AI buildout — the market is that large. We think we have structural advantages: our breadth of services, our custom silicon providing better price/performance, the Bedrock catalog's breadth, and our enterprise relationships. Azure has advantages in Microsoft-centric enterprise environments. We are not worried about the size of the pie."

**Analyst question on FTC antitrust case:** "How are you managing the legal uncertainty from the FTC case?"

**Andy Jassy:** "We disagree with the FTC's characterization of our business practices. We believe we operate in the interest of both consumers and sellers. The marketplace is highly competitive and sellers have many options. We will defend ourselves vigorously in the legal process."

**Analyst question on capex and margin pressure:** "With capex at $100 billion for 2025, how should we think about free cash flow and margin trajectory?"

**Brian Olsavsky:** "High capex is a sign of confidence in demand. The AI infrastructure we are building today will serve customers for 15-20 years. We are in the early innings of the cloud and AI buildout, and underinvesting now would be the bigger mistake. Margins will absorb some pressure near term, but we are managing it carefully."
